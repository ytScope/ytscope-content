# Channel management

The My Channels page lets you manage all your tracked YouTube channels in one place. Add channels, assign roles, apply filters, and compare competitors side by side.

<Screenshot
  src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ytScope/ytscope-content/main/images/academy/en/channels-list.webp"
  alt="My Channels page overview"
  caption="My Channels page — all tracked channels displayed in a grid layout."
/>

The page has two tabs:

| Tab | Purpose |
|-----|---------|
| **Channels** | List, filter, and manage your tracked channels. |
| **Compare** | Compare selected channels by metrics side by side. |

---

## Channels tab

This tab displays all channels you're tracking. The toolbar at the top provides filtering and channel management controls.

### Filters

Use the filter buttons at the top to narrow the channel list by role.

| Filter | Channels shown |
|--------|----------------|
| **All** | Every tracked channel. |
| **Own** | Only your channel marked as **"Own"**. |
| **Competitor** | Only channels marked as **"Competitor"**. |

Next to the filter buttons, the **channel limit indicator** shows your usage. For example, **12/50** means you've used 12 of your 50 channel slots.

### Channel cards

Each channel appears as a card. The card displays the following information:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Avatar and name** | Channel profile image and name. Click to open the channel detail page. |
| **Handle** | The channel's YouTube username (e.g., @dopinghafiza). |
| **Role badge** | Shows the channel's relation type: **Own**, **Competitor**, or **Watchlist**. |
| **Subscribers** | Current subscriber count and 7-day change percentage. |
| **Total views** | The channel's total view count and trend percentage. |
| **Sparkline** | A small trend chart that visually summarizes subscriber and view changes. |

The colored line on the left edge of the card indicates the trend: green for growth, red for decline, gray for no change.

<Callout type="tip">
Click the three-dot menu (⋯) on any card to change the channel's relation type or remove it from tracking.
</Callout>

## Adding a channel

Click the **"+ Add Channel"** button in the top-right corner to add a new channel.

<Screenshot
  src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ytScope/ytscope-content/main/images/academy/en/channels-add-modal.webp"
  alt="Add Channel modal"
  caption="Add Channel modal — search, select a role, and add."
/>

<Step number={1} title="Search for a channel">
Type a channel name, @handle, or YouTube URL into the search box. Results appear automatically after you enter at least 3 characters.
</Step>

<Step number={2} title="Select a role">
Choose the role for the channel:

| Role | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Own** | Your YouTube channel. Only 1 channel can have this role. |
| **Competitor** | Competitor channels you want to monitor. |
| **Watchlist** | Channels you follow for inspiration or reference. |
</Step>

<Step number={3} title="Add the channel">
Select the channel from the search results. It's added to your list and data loads within a few minutes.
</Step>

<Callout type="note">
If the channel doesn't appear in search results, use the **"Add by URL or ID"** option. You can paste a YouTube channel URL directly.
</Callout>

<ProTip>
Add your own channel with the **"Own"** role. This way, the comparison tab highlights your performance relative to competitors.
</ProTip>

## Compare tab

The Compare tab lets you analyze your tracked channels side by side using key metrics. Comparison sections appear once you select at least 2 channels.

<Screenshot
  src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ytScope/ytscope-content/main/images/academy/en/comparison-selector.webp"
  alt="Compare tab — channel selector"
  caption="Compare tab — select channels, set the period and metric."
/>

### Channel selection and controls

Three control areas sit at the top of the page:

| Control | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| **Channel cards** | Select channels to compare. You can choose up to **5 channels**. |
| **Period selector** | Comparison period: **7 days**, **30 days**, **90 days**, or **365 days**. |
| **Metric selector** | Chart metric: **Subscribers**, **Views**, or **Engagement**. |

Quick selection buttons speed up the process:

| Button | Action |
|--------|--------|
| **All Competitors** | Selects all competitor channels in one click. |
| **Top 3** | Selects the top 3 channels by subscriber count. |

Your own channel stands out with a **"You"** badge. It always appears as the reference point in comparison results.

### Comparison sections

Once you select 2 or more channels, the following analysis sections appear:

| Section | What it shows |
|---------|---------------|
| **Comparison cards** | Summarizes each channel's subscribers, views, and video count. The top performer is marked with a crown icon. |
| **Growth chart** | Time-series chart for the selected metric — view growth curves for all channels at once. |
| **Growth momentum** | Each channel's growth acceleration and trend direction. |
| **Content strategy** | Upload frequency, average views per video, and content type distribution (Shorts vs. regular). |
| **Engagement** | Engagement rate, average likes, and comments per video. |
| **Breakout analysis** | Detects videos that performed far above the channel's average. |
| **Upload time** | Heatmap showing the most effective upload day and hour. |
| **Video duration** | Average view performance by duration category. |
| **Title keywords** | Frequency and performance analysis of the most-used title keywords. |
| **Best videos** | Compares the most-viewed videos from each channel. |

<Callout type="tip">
When you change the period selector, all sections update automatically. Compare 30-day and 90-day results to distinguish short-term spikes from sustained trends.
</Callout>

<ProTip>
Your channel is highlighted with a **"You"** badge in comparison results. Quickly spot the metrics where you lag behind competitors and adjust your content strategy accordingly.
</ProTip>
