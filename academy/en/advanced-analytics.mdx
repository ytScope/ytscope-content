# Advanced analytics

The Advanced tab goes beyond standard metrics to offer deep insights into channel performance. It includes 10 analysis sections ranging from market share to upload time optimization, title keywords to revenue estimation.

  caption="Advanced tab — Share of Voice (SOV) and period analysis sections."
/>

Click the **Advanced** tab on the channel detail page to access this section. All analyses are calculated based on the selected time period.

---

## Share of Voice (SOV)

Share of Voice shows the channel's position among all your tracked channels. It provides three SOV metrics:

| Metric | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Views SOV** | The channel's share of total views. For example, 20.9% means roughly one-fifth of all tracked views belong to this channel. |
| **Subscribers SOV** | The channel's share of total subscribers. |
| **Growth SOV** | The channel's share of growth during the period. |

The status badge (Healthy, Attention, Critical) and trend direction (Rising, Declining) at the top provide a quick summary. The rank value (e.g., **2/12**) shows the channel's position among tracked channels.

<Callout type="tip">
Channels with low Views SOV but high Growth SOV are on an upward trend. It's worth examining what they're doing differently.
</Callout>

## View concentration

View Concentration uses Pareto analysis to show how views are distributed across videos.

| Metric | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Top 10%** | Share of total views held by the top 10% most-viewed videos. |
| **Top 20%** | Share of total views held by the top 20%. |
| **Top 50%** | Share of total views held by the top half. |

Results are compared against the 80/20 rule. High concentration means a few videos carry most of the views. A balanced distribution indicates many videos are performing well.

## Upload time analysis

A heatmap showing upload performance by day and hour. The 7-day × 24-hour grid uses color intensity to represent the average views of videos uploaded in each time slot.

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Dark cell** | High average views — videos uploaded at this time performed better. |
| **Light cell** | Lower average views. |
| **Best slot** | The highlighted cell marks the day-hour combination with the highest average views. |

<ProTip>
Identify your best upload time from the heatmap and publish new content in that time slot. Consistency also benefits the algorithm.
</ProTip>

## Engagement benchmark

Compares the channel's engagement rate against the YouTube average. The general YouTube average of **3.87%** is used as the reference.

| Metric | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Average engagement** | The channel's average engagement rate. |
| **YouTube average** | Industry-wide reference value (3.87%). |
| **Median** | Median engagement rate across videos — unaffected by outliers. |
| **Min / Max** | Videos with the lowest and highest engagement rates. |
| **Shorts vs Regular** | Engagement rate comparison between short and long-form videos. |

---

## Seasonal trends

Shows the channel's performance cycles on a monthly or quarterly basis. Recurring increases or decreases in views or engagement during certain periods appear in this section.

<Callout type="note">
Seasonal trends require at least 90 days of data for meaningful results. Newly added channels may show limited data in this section.
</Callout>

---

## Title keywords

Analyzes words used in video titles. Contains three sub-tabs:

| Tab | Content |
|-----|---------|
| **Keywords** | Most frequently used words, their usage count, and average view performance per keyword. |
| **Title length** | Performance distribution by title character count — whether short or long titles perform better. |
| **Naming patterns** | Common structures in titles (questions, lists, how-to, etc.) and their performance. |

<ProTip>
Include the keywords with the highest average views in your future video titles.
</ProTip>

---

## Video duration analysis

Shows performance by video duration category.

| Category | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Shorts** | Videos under 60 seconds. |
| **Medium** | Videos between 1–15 minutes. |
| **Long** | Videos between 15–60 minutes. |
| **Extra long** | Videos over 60 minutes. |

Each category lists video count, average views, and engagement rate. Identify which duration range performs best on the channel.

---

## Video age analysis

Shows how long videos continue to receive views after publication.

| View | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Engagement trend** | How engagement rate changes over time since publication. |
| **Content age distribution** | Whether new videos or older (evergreen) content carries most of the views. |

A high evergreen content ratio indicates the channel draws significant traffic from search.

---

## Breakout and anomaly detection

Statistically identifies videos that performed far above normal (breakouts). Uses z-score analysis to flag videos that deviate from the channel average.

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Breakout videos** | Videos with significantly higher views than the channel average. |
| **Anomalies** | Unexpected positive or negative performance deviations. |
| **Patterns** | Common traits among breakout videos (title structure, duration, topic, etc.). |

<Callout type="tip">
Study what your breakout videos have in common — topic, title structure, and thumbnail style. Apply those traits to future content.
</Callout>

---

## Revenue estimate

Displays the channel's estimated revenue. Values are based on CPM (cost per thousand views) and region-based estimates.

| Metric | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Gross revenue** | Total estimated revenue including YouTube's share. |
| **Net revenue** | Estimated amount the creator receives. |
| **RPM** | Estimated earnings per thousand views (Revenue Per Mille). |
| **YouTube share** | Percentage taken by YouTube (typically 45%). |

<Callout type="warning">
Revenue estimates are approximate. Actual earnings vary based on ad type, viewer region, niche, and sponsorship deals.
</Callout>
