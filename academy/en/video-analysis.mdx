The **Videos** page lets you find and sort content from tracked channels, select videos for comparison, and evaluate one video in its channel context.

## Outcome, prerequisites, and path

- **Path:** choose **Videos** in the main navigation; select a card for detail.
- You need at least one tracked, synced channel.
- The top period and channel selection define the **List** and **Top** result set. Video detail has a separate period selector.
- Your plan determines available periods and comparison selections; the on-screen limit is authoritative.

## Screen map

1. **List:** filters, sorting, pagination, and selection.
2. **Top:** leaders by views, likes, comments, and engagement.
3. **Compare:** analysis of videos selected from List.
4. Video detail header and summary metrics.
5. Performance grade, daily chart, velocity, and engagement.
6. Channel rank/percentile and SEO score.

## List tab

| Control | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Period** | Includes videos published in the selected window. |
| **Search** | Searches video titles; clear it when no result matches. |
| **Channel** | All tracked channels or one channel. It may be hidden when only one exists. |
| **All / Regular / Shorts** | Format filter. |
| **Date / Views / Likes / Engagement** | Sort metric. |
| **Descending / Ascending** | Sort direction. |
| **Previous / Next** | Result pages; changing a filter returns to page one. |

A card shows thumbnail, duration/Shorts badge, title, channel, views, likes, comments, and publish age. Counts are current cumulative values; the period can control which videos qualify rather than truncating each total.

### Comparison selection

The card checkbox adds a video to the bottom selection bar. The bar shows selected count, **Clear**, and **Compare**. At least two videos are required; the upper bound is your live plan's comparison limit. Do not infer it from a general counter on another page. Verify retained selections in the bar after filtering or paging.

| **Views** | Cumulative view count. |

The first three appear on the podium and the rest in a ranked list. Period and channel filter define the cohort. An engagement leader need not be the views leader; distinguish counts from rates.

The header shows thumbnail, duration, Shorts badge, publish age, channel/handle, and **Watch on YouTube**. The selector can hide windows longer than the video's age; when a hint appears, use the product's valid period set.

### Summary metrics and percentage language

| Card | Scope |
|---|---|
| **Views / Likes / Comments** | Current cumulative count; change compares first and last snapshot in the selected detail period. |
| **Engagement** | Current rate, percent. |
| **Views/Day** | Cumulative views divided by lifetime age; not last-24-hour velocity. |

`20% growth` is relative to the starting value. If engagement moves from `4.0%` to `5.0%`, the difference is **1 percentage point**, while relative growth is **25%**. When the UI reports a difference versus channel average in points, do not call it percent growth.

### Performance grade and subscores

The overall 0–100 score and **A+–F** grade are a product heuristic combining:

| Subscore | Input |
|---|---|
| **View Performance** | Current views relative to channel average. |
| **Engagement** | Video engagement rate relative to channel average. |
| **Velocity** | Available 24/48-hour movement and trend. |
| **SEO** | Title, description, tag, and thumbnail subscores. |

This is not a YouTube algorithm quality score. Formula and thresholds can change; read the raw metric with the subscore. A new or sparsely tracked video can have limited velocity evidence.

### Daily performance chart

- **X-axis:** date.
- **Left Y-axis / area:** view count.
- **Right Y-axis / bars:** like and comment counts.
- **Dashed reference:** average views when enough days exist.
- **Tooltip:** date, views, likes, and comments.

With fewer than two daily records, channel-average comparison replaces the chart. Missing days are not connected. Do not compare area and bar heights directly because they use different axes.

### Video vs channel average

Each row shows video value and channel average. The middle marker is the average; the text ratio is `video value ÷ channel average × 100`. `150%` means 1.5 times the average, not “150% more.” The channel average can mix ages and formats.

### View velocity and 48-hour availability

| Field | Unit / prerequisite |
|---|---|
| **Last 24h / Last 48h** | View gain derived from eligible snapshots, count. |
| **Daily Rate** | Views/day normalized from observed hours. |
| **Trend** | Accelerating/decelerating/stable when two comparable windows exist. |

The card is hidden when all values are zero or unavailable. Use the tooltip and video age to separate no data from true zero. For a video younger than 48 hours, the 48-hour field is not a completed outcome.

### Channel rank and percentile

In `#rank / total videos`, **#1 is best**. Views, likes, comments, and engagement have separate ranks. The displayed percentage follows `rank ÷ total × 100`; therefore a **lower percentage means a better rank**. A `0%` or very small value is not a poor score. Always report percentage with rank and total.

### SEO score

The 0–100 SEO score is a product heuristic using visible factors:

- title length;
- description presence/length;
- tag count;
- custom thumbnail use.

The card shows category, a subset of tags, and each factor's current ideal text. It does not guarantee search rank or views and does not measure topic demand, audience intent, title quality, or watch behavior.

## States and troubleshooting

| State | Action |
|---|---|
| **List loading** | Wait for the skeleton before selecting. |
| **No results** | Clear or widen search, channel, format, and period filters. |
| **Error** | Refresh; retry later if it persists. |
| **Detail missing/inaccessible** | Confirm the video belongs to an active tracked channel. |
| **No chart** | Wait for at least two snapshots; use channel comparison meanwhile. |
| **No velocity** | Check video age and 24/48-hour snapshot availability. |
| **Compare disabled** | Select at least two and no more than the live plan limit. |

## Interpretation limits

- A period filter can select the cohort without truncating cumulative card values.
- Comparing videos with very different ages or formats by raw totals is not fair.
- Grade, SEO, and percentile are product definitions, not official YouTube quality signals.
- Private YouTube Studio traffic-source and retention data is not present.
